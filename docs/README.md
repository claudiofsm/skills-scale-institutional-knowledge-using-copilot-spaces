# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Docs. This folder contains the core process guidance used by OctoAcme teams to deliver work consistently, transparently, and with clear accountability across product, engineering, QA, and stakeholder groups.

OctoAcme uses a lightweight, structured project management model that moves through initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Across all phases, teams emphasize customer value, iterative delivery, clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety. Core roles are clearly defined: Project Managers coordinate delivery and communication, Product Managers define outcomes and priorities, Developers implement and test solutions, QA validates acceptance criteria, and stakeholders provide input and approvals.

Projects start with initiation to validate business need, identify stakeholders, define measurable success criteria, and decide whether work should proceed. From there, planning turns approved initiatives into shippable increments through a prioritized backlog, acceptance criteria, estimation, Definition of Done, dependency mapping, and release milestones. Risk management starts early and continues through delivery using a simple risk register with named ownership, mitigation plans, and regular review.

During execution, teams use visible workflow management and a predictable cadence (standups, delivery syncs, demos) to keep progress on track. Delivery practices favor small pull requests linked to issues and acceptance criteria, with CI quality checks such as tests, linting, and security scanning. Release and deployment follow checklist-based controls, and retrospectives after sprints, releases, or incidents feed action items back into the backlog, creating a continuous improvement loop.
